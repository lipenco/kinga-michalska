  • Project Dance

    Documentation of contact improv and contemporary dance performances, workshops and festivals.

  • Humanature

    I was trying to capture the relationship between people and pure, natural, open spaces. Are humans an integral part of these landscapes or are they intruders?

  • Project Rehab

    Series of portraits of men from a Colombian rehab for drug addicts. Aged between 16 and 30. Thieves, killers, soldiers, drug dealers, strippers, transvestites.

  • Mama Dances

    Mama Dances is a dance activity for mothers who want to dance with their babies. The school is based in Montreal and was started by a dance artist and a choreographer Eryn Dace Trudell.

  • Dreamers

    People sleeping in public places

  • Family

    Family photo shoots of young farmers and their children form BC and Nova Scotia, Canada.

  • Femme

    This documentary project is my private exploration of feminine view on feminine body. It shows a struggle between wanting to be free and natural and at the same time having to fit in to the society, where people have a clear sense of what does it mean to be a woman and how she should look like.

  • Studio

    Studio portraits of women from Poland in their late '30 and early '40. The goal of this project is to make women feel beautiful and feminine, and to help them accept their bodies. I use the camera to show my models that there are many ways of looking at their body and one of them is to look at it with love and respect.

  • Inner landscape

    This is a collection of textures that I gathered throughout my travels all over the globe. I am looking for images hidden in nature which reflected my emotions.

Hi!

My name is Kinga Michalska.I am a photographer and a traveller from Poland.

I graduated form Warsaw School of Photography in 2010 and from Cultural Studies at Warsaw University in 2012. I use my camera to tell other people's stories, to show their emotions and to make them notice the beauty that I see in them.

I travelled and photographed in India, Nepal, Colombia, Peru, Bolivia, Ecuador, Canada, US, Eastern and Western Europe. Wherever I go I like to meet ordinary people, take pictures of them, their families, their everyday life. I love to work with children, because of the honest and spontaneous way in which they behave.

I am particularly interested in the themes of femininity, body and movement. I treat photography as a magical tool which allows me to meet interesting people and to create unique bounds.
